# Legend of the Burning Sands: How to Play (the Human Version)

*A friendly, plain-language guide to the 1998 Arabian-fantasy CCG — built from the official rulebook, illustrated with real cards. Don't worry about memorizing any of this. Read it once, deal out some cards, and it'll click fast.*

> 📖 **Want the source of truth?** Read the [**official Legend of the Burning Sands rulebook (PDF)**](https://assets.ccgvault.io/rulebooks/legend-of-the-burning-sands.pdf) anytime — this guide is the friendly companion to it.

## 1. What is this game, really?

Forget the dense rulebook for a second. Here's the pitch:

You are a **power broker in a dying mythic city** — Medinaat al-Salaam, the **Jewel of the Desert**, a sprawling Arabian metropolis (also called the City of One Thousand Stories) in the last days before an age of fire and ruin. You command one of twelve **Factions** — imperial Egyptians, sorcerer-assassins, Roman legions, immortal star-gazers, death cults, merchant princes — and you fight to come out on top before the end.

You win by being the last one standing, by telling enough legendary **Stories**, or by pulling off a special victory your cards set up. Along the way you'll hire **Heroes**, arm them, cast **Spells**, summon **Jinn**, raid your rivals' wells at night, and settle grudges with cinematic **duels**.

Three things sit at the heart of everything:

- 💧 **Water** is *life*. Run out and you're eliminated. It also pays for your most powerful cards.
- 🪙 **Copper** is *money*. It hires Heroes and buys gear.
- 💜 **Influence** is *politics* — the clout to pull strings and command the city's council.

Almost every decision is really *"how do I spend Water, Copper, and Influence right now without leaving myself exposed?"*

## 2. The Twelve Factions of the Jewel

This is the heart of the game's flavor. By the end of its run, Legend of the Burning Sands had **twelve factions**, each a distinct culture fighting for the city's soul — and each one is represented by a real Stronghold in our card database. Here's who they are, how they play, and a look at their cards.

## 3. The three resources you're always juggling

**💧 Water — your lifeline.** Your Water sits as tokens on your **City Sections**. You spend it to bring powerful cards into play, and — crucially — **if you ever end your turn with zero Water anywhere, you're knocked out of the game.** Water is both your fuel *and* your health bar. Treat it like blood.

**🪙 Copper — your wallet.** You make Copper by **bowing** (tilting sideways — think "tapping") a Copper-producing card like your Stronghold or a Holding. There's a catch the game calls the *Sultan's tax*: you can only pay for **one card at a time**, and any Copper you produce but don't spend is **lost**. So you don't bank Copper between purchases — you generate exactly what you need, when you need it.

**💜 Influence — your political pull.** Heroes and your Stronghold each have an Influence value. You spend it (by bowing those cards) to take **Council actions** and to afford Heroes from factions other than your own. It's the quiet resource — but in a game about a scheming city, quiet power wins games.

## 4. Your home base: Stronghold + City Sections

Before the game even starts, you set up your **home** — the table area in front of you.

### Your Stronghold — who you are

![Doomed City of Laramun — a Stronghold card](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/qgl45lpln4gc0ss8.jpg)

Your Stronghold is the foundation of your whole game. It never goes into your deck and **can't be destroyed**. It tells you:

- **Your Faction** (shown by the icon near the top).
- **City Points** (green hexagon) — a one-time budget you spend at setup to buy your City Sections.
- **Copper Production** (orange) — how much Copper it makes when you bow it.
- **Influence** (purple chalice).
- A **special ability**, and a **Fate value** in the triangle at the bottom.

*(That's Dahab's Stronghold above — note the huge City Points and the ability that frees you from Faction restrictions on City Sections, exactly the merchant-toolbox flavor from the factions section.)*

### City Sections — your wells and your turf

![Ancient Pillars of Qaharaba — a City Section card](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/ckswa0g6qe8g0kwk.jpg)

City Sections are the neighborhoods you control — and they're where your **Water** lives. At setup you spend your Stronghold's City Points to put some into play. Each one shows:

- **Base Strength** (top-left) — how much damage it can soak before it's destroyed.
- **Water Capacity** (top-right water drop) — how much Water it can hold.
- **City Points cost** (the third number) — what it costs to deploy at setup.
- Plus any special text, and sometimes the **Advanced** trait.

Here's the tension that drives the whole game: **your Water — your life — is sitting out in the open on these City Sections, and your opponents can attack them and raid them.** Lose all your City Sections (or all your Water) and you're done.

## 5. The cards you'll be holding — a guided tour

Your deck (at least **55 cards**, max **3 copies** of any one card) is built from these types. Here's one real example of each, and where to find the numbers that matter.

### 🦅 Hero — your main characters

![Abd al-Zhayn — a Hero card](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/915clpf5ts00sows.jpg)

Heroes do the heavy lifting: they fight, defend, raid, duel, and cast spells. Reading a Hero:

- **Sun globe, top-left = Strength** (how hard they hit). Here, 3.
- **Moon globe, top-right = Ka** (their inner life force). If a Hero's Ka ever hits 0, **they die.** Here, 3.
- The three values under the art: **Water cost**, **Copper cost**, **Influence** (1 / 7 / 4 here).
- The text box holds **traits** (like "Unaligned Wanderer") and abilities, plus italic flavor text.
- **Fate value** in the bottom triangle (used for duels, raids, and deciding who goes first).

### 🧍 Follower — sidekicks who buff a Hero

![Advanced Scout — a Follower card](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/not7b1txkn44gkgc.jpg)

Followers attach to a Hero and make them better. Their Strength/Ka often show a **"+"** because they *add* to the Hero they're stapled to. (This Scout adds 0/0 but brings a great ability: no Omens can be played in a battle it's part of.) A Hero plus everything attached to them is called a **unit**.

### ⚔️ Item — gear for your Heroes

![Ancestral Sword of the Kirin — an Item card](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/1y950q1069ogsk0g.jpg)

Items are weapons, armor, and magical objects you attach to a Hero for a Strength and/or Ka bonus (the **+1** here), plus special abilities. Note the bold traits — **Weapon • Unique** — and the rule that a Hero can carry only one Weapon and one piece of Armor at a time.

### ✨ Spell — magic, cast by Sahir

![Afshin — a Spell card (a Jinn)](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/dg2jgels7m88s0c4.jpg)

Spells are cast by bowing one of your **Sahir** (sorcerer) Heroes. Afshin here is a **Jinn** spell — casting it summons a Jinn Hero (a creature of "smokeless fire") right onto the table. Jinn fight like any Hero but have 0 Influence and stick around even after the spell resolves. (The Qabal and Celestial Alliance factions are built around exactly this.)

### 🎬 Action — one-off plays

![A Better World — an Action card](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/ecns81313z4k40kg.jpg)

Actions are things your people *do* — you play one, its effect happens, and it's discarded. The key is **when** you can play it, shown by a keyword in the text:

- **Day** — only during the Day Phase.
- **Night** — only during the Night Phase.
- **Battle** — only during a battle.
- **Open** — any of the above.
- **Reaction** — in response to something, whenever its trigger happens.

### 🏛️ Holding — your resource engines

![Abdul-Rafi's — a Holding card](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/gldld9alzm04g04c.jpg)

Holdings are the cards that *generate* Water and Copper, or grant handy abilities. They enter play **bowed** (so they can't do anything the turn they arrive). A special subtype, **Fortifications**, attach to your City Sections to make them tougher.

### 📖 Story — win by telling tales

![A Dying Sahir's Tale — a Story card](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/drliub35oh4ow4gs.jpg)

Stories are how you win *narratively*. Each shows **Story Points** and a **"Tale Reaction"** (the condition that lets you play it) and a **"Reward"** (the bonus you get while it's in play). **Get 5 Story Points' worth of Stories into play and you win on the spot** — a Story Victory.

### 🔱 Pantheon — the favor of a god

![Conquest — a Pantheon card](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/ch0kuld18wocs0g8.jpg)

Pantheon cards represent the deity your Faction worships (Conquest is the Yodatai war-god). You can only run **one of each** in your deck, and only one can be in play at a time — play a new one and the old god is buried. Gods are fickle.

## 6. How a turn flows

Every turn moves through four phases. The player with the **Caliph's Blessing** acts first each phase (it passes left each turn).

**🌅 1. Dawn Phase** — Straighten all your bowed cards (untap everything). Fresh start.

**☀️ 2. Day Phase** — The busy one. On your turns you can:
- Play **Day** or **Open** actions.
- Bring a **Hero** or **Holding** into play (paying Water + Copper).
- Attach a **Follower** or **Item** to one of your unbowed Heroes.
- Move attached cards between your Heroes.
- **Once per turn, declare an attack** (see the next section).

**🌙 3. Night Phase** — The sneaky one:
- Play **Night** or **Open** actions.
- **Once per turn, declare a raid** to steal Water.
- Pay 3 Water to bring a dead **Khadi** Hero back, bowed.

**🌑 4. End Phase** — Cleanup:
- Anyone with no Water left is eliminated.
- Draw back up to your hand size (**4 cards, +1 per City Section** you control).
- Put fresh Water tokens onto your City Sections.
- Pass the Caliph's Blessing to your left.

A note on **bowing**: turning a card sideways means "it has acted this turn." Bowed cards can't attack, defend, raid, or use their text — they wake back up at Dawn.

## 7. Going to war

Combat in LBS is about attacking your opponents' **City Sections** — because that's where their Water (life) sits.

When you declare an attack, it runs in steps:

1. **Declaration** — You name which enemy City Sections you're hitting and commit your unbowed Hero units. The defender (and even other players, as **allies**) can answer.
2. **Ground maneuvers**, then **3. Flying maneuvers** — Units come in two flavors. A unit is **Ground** by default; if a Hero *and* all their attached Followers have the **Flying** trait, the unit is **Flying** and commits in its own step (a great way to slip past ground defenders).
4. **Battle actions** — Now the fighting happens, one City Section at a time.

The core combat verb is **engage**: bow one of your Heroes in the battle to deal damage equal to its **Strength**. The opposing player must **absorb** that damage — by destroying their own cards (each card soaks damage equal to its Strength), popping Water tokens (1 point each), or letting the City Section itself take the hit. Destroy all a City Section's Water and then its Base Strength, and it's gone.

A couple of flavorful traits shape battles:
- **Archery** lets you aim your engage damage at a *single* chosen enemy card instead of letting them choose what to sacrifice. (The Ivory Kingdoms live here.)
- **Berserk** forces the opponent to absorb with *everything* — no holding cards back.

## 8. Duels — the cinematic showdown

Some cards let one Hero **challenge** another to a one-on-one duel. If the challenge is accepted, it plays out like a tense sword-fight:

- The attacker **thrusts** by placing a card face-down — its **Fate value** is the strength of the blow.
- The defender **must parry**, either off the top of their deck or from hand, also face-down.
- Reveal both. Compare Fate values: a parry that's too weak lets the thrust through, costing the defending Hero **Ka**. Over-commit and you "miss." (The **Duelist** trait quietly adds +1 to your parry.)
- Then roles swap — defender becomes attacker — and it continues until someone passes or a Hero's **Ka hits 0 and dies.**

It's a beautiful little game-within-the-game, and Story cards like *A Dying Sahir's Tale* (above) reward you for winning duels against stronger foes.

---

## 9. Raiding — stealing Water in the night

Since Water is life and you never quite have enough, the Night Phase lets you **raid** an opponent's City Section to take some:

1. Declare the raid and assign an unbowed Hero, placing a card **face-down** on it — that card's **Fate value** is your raid score.
2. The defender assigns a Hero with a **face-up** card.
3. Compare Fate values. Beat the defender (or the City Section's Base Strength, if undefended) and your Hero **carries off 1 Water token** (more with the **Carry +X** trait).

Raids don't destroy anything — they just bleed your rival's lifeline while topping up your own. This is the Moto faction's whole reason for living.

---

## 10. How to win

There's more than one road to victory:

- **🗡️ Military Victory** — Eliminate everyone else (destroy their City Sections, or starve them of Water).
- **📜 Story Victory** — Get **5 Story Points** of Story cards into play. Instant win.
- **🌟 Alternate Victories** — Certain Story cards define their own special win conditions; once you have them in play, that path opens up just for you.

And remember the ever-present loss condition: **end any turn with no Water, and you're out.**

## 11. Seeing it in action: interactions & a sample turn

Rules read dry; the game plays lively. Here are a few quick exchanges, then a full turn narrated back-and-forth.

### Quick interactions

**An engage, absorbed.** Your Hero (Strength 4) engages in a battle at your rival's City Section. That's 4 damage they must absorb. They sacrifice a bowed 1-Strength Follower (soaks 1) and pop 3 of the City Section's Water tokens (1 each). Damage covered — but they're now three Water poorer, and that's three steps closer to the edge.

**An Archery snipe.** An Ivory Kingdoms archer engages with **Archery**. Instead of letting the defender choose what to sacrifice, the archer points at the enemy's key Sahir and forces *that* card to eat the damage. The spellcaster dies; the rest of the unit is untouched but suddenly toothless.

**A Jinn, summoned.** During your Day Phase you bow a Sahir and cast *Afshin*. A Jinn of Earthly Pleasures (Strength 1 / Ka 3, **Flying**) pops into play. Next turn he can slip past ground defenders in the Flying Maneuvers step — and his Jinn Open ability can even fish you a Water token.

![Afshin — a Jinn Spell](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/dg2jgels7m88s0c4.jpg) ![A Dying Sahir's Tale — a Story rewarding duel wins](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/drliub35oh4ow4gs.jpg)

*Afshin (Jinn Spell) · A Dying Sahir's Tale (Story)*

**A duel to the death.** Your 4-Ka Khadi challenges a rival's 3-Ka Hero. You thrust a face-down 3-Fate card; they blind-parry off the top of their deck and flip a 2. The parry falls short by 1 — their Hero loses a Ka, dropping to 2. Roles swap, they thrust back… and if their Hero ever hits 0 Ka, it dies. Win the duel and you might even trigger *A Dying Sahir's Tale* (above) for Story points.

### A full turn, narrated

Let's slow right down and play a full round as if you've never touched a card. Two players sit across from each other:

- **You** play **Senpet** — the old empire; disciplined daytime soldiers.
- **Rashida** plays **Moto** — the horse-raiders; she steals by night.

You hold the **Caliph's Blessing** this round, so you act first in every phase.

#### The board before anyone acts

Each of you has already set up your **home**: a Stronghold plus at least one City Section with Water sitting on it.

![The Empire of the Senpet — your Stronghold](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/35sn81laweecows0.jpg) ![Senpet Garrison — your City Section](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/s0495x2bkuocs8sw.jpg)

*Your side: The Empire of the Senpet (Stronghold) and your Senpet Garrison (City Section).*

Your side of the table:
- **The Empire of the Senpet** — your Stronghold. It can't be destroyed. When you **bow** it (tilt it sideways to show it's been used this turn), it produces **Copper**, your spending money.
- **Senpet Garrison** — a City Section. Its top-right number is **Water Capacity 7**, and right now it holds **5 Water tokens**. That Water *is your life total* — end a turn with zero Water anywhere and you're eliminated.
- One Hero already standing from an earlier turn: **Abd al-Zhayn** (**Strength 3 / Ka 3**), currently **unbowed** (upright and ready).

In hand you're holding a **Crysteel Sword** (a Weapon you can attach to a Hero) and a few cards your opponent can't see. Across the table, Rashida has her **Moto Oasis** Stronghold, a City Section with **5 Water** of her own, and her champion raider waiting in hand.

#### 🌅 Dawn Phase — wake everything up

Dawn is pure bookkeeping: **straighten** (un-tilt) every bowed card from last turn. Now everything you own is upright and ready. (On the very first turn there's nothing to straighten — but every turn after, this is how your Stronghold and Heroes "recharge.")

#### ☀️ Day Phase — build, then attack

This is where you actually act, one step at a time, until you pass. Your plan: arm your Hero, then hit Rashida's well.

**1) Make money.** You **bow The Empire of the Senpet** to produce its **Copper** — let's say **7 Copper**. Two things every new player needs burned into memory right here:
- Bowing it means it's *spent for the turn* — it won't make more until next Dawn.
- You can only pay for **one card at a time**, and any Copper you don't spend on that card **vanishes** (the flavor calls it the "Sultan's tax"). Copper is use-it-or-lose-it, never banked.

**2) Arm your Hero.** You attach the **Crysteel Sword** to Abd al-Zhayn. Its cost is **0 Water / 4 Copper**, so you pay 4 of your 7 Copper and the other 3 evaporate (there's the tax in action). The sword grants **+1 Strength / +1 Ka**, so Abd al-Zhayn is now a **Strength 4 / Ka 4** fighter.

![Abd al-Zhayn — your Hero, base Strength 3 / Ka 3](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/915clpf5ts00sows.jpg) ![Crysteel Sword — a Weapon granting +1S / +1K](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/skcnuy25byoc44wk.jpg)

*Abd al-Zhayn, now wielding the Crysteel Sword → Strength 4 / Ka 4.*

*(Notice you can't* also *hire a fresh Hero this turn — you've already bowed your only money-maker. Adding a second Copper source, like a **Holding**, is exactly how players ramp toward bigger turns.)*

**3) Declare an attack.** You point Abd al-Zhayn at Rashida's City Section — her well. Attacks run in a short sequence:
- **Declaration:** you announce you're attacking that City Section.
- **Ground maneuvers:** Abd al-Zhayn has no **Flying** trait, so he's a *Ground* unit and commits now. Rashida assigns a defender — she puts up **Moto Marik** (Strength 2 / Ka 1) to block.
- **Battle:** now the actual fighting, which the game calls **engaging**.

**4) Engage.** You **bow Abd al-Zhayn to engage**, dealing damage equal to his **Strength (4)** to Rashida's side. She must **absorb all 4**, and she chooses how: destroy her own cards in the battle (each soaks damage equal to *its* Strength), pop **Water tokens** off the City Section (1 point each), or let the City Section itself be destroyed.

Rashida won't throw away Marik, so she **pops 4 Water tokens** to soak the hit. Her well drops from 5 Water to **1**. You didn't break any of her cards — but you bled four points of her *life* and left her on the brink. The battle ends and everyone in it goes home **bowed**.

You've done what you wanted, so you pass into the night.

#### 🌙 Night Phase — Rashida raids

Night belongs to the raiders, and Rashida is Moto. Where your attack tried to *destroy*, a **raid** tries to *steal Water* — exactly what she needs after you just drained her.

![Moto Marik — the Moto Raid Master, Carry +2](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/bwu2glat614400wc.jpg) ![Moto Steeds — a Follower granting Raid +1](https://assets.ccgvault.io/cards/legend-of-the-burning-sands-ccg/mn9ijpql2zkkw8oo.jpg)

*Moto Marik (Carry +2 — and he gets to assign* after *you commit your defenders) with Moto Steeds attached (Raid +1).*

She assigns **Moto Marik** to raid your **Senpet Garrison**. Here's how a raid resolves:
- Rashida slides a card **face-down** onto Marik. That hidden card's **Fate value** (the number in the bottom triangle) is her raid score. Marik also carries **Moto Steeds**, a Follower granting **Raid +1**. Say her face-down card is Fate **3**; +1 from the Steeds makes her raid score **4**.
- You **defend** by assigning a Hero with a card placed **face-up**; its Fate value is your defense score. You flip a **2**.
- **Compare:** her **4** beats your **2**, so the raid succeeds. A normal raid steals just **1 Water** — but Marik is the Raid Master with **Carry +2**, so he hauls off **3 Water tokens** from your Garrison to her Oasis.

Your Senpet Garrison was untouched during your own daytime attack, so it still held 5 Water — now Marik steals 3, dropping you to **2**, while Rashida's well climbs back up. The hunter just became the hunted.

#### 🌑 End Phase — clean up and reset

The round winds down:
- **Elimination check:** anyone at **zero Water** is knocked out. You have 2, Rashida has at least 1 — both safe.
- **Draw:** refill your hand to your hand size (**4 cards + 1 per City Section**).
- **Replenish Water:** you each place fresh Water tokens onto your City Sections. *This is the pressure valve that stops the game being a quick death-spiral* — wells refill every turn, so winning is about out-draining your opponent over time, not landing one big hit.
- **Pass the Blessing:** the Caliph's Blessing slides to your left, so Rashida acts first next round.

#### So what actually happened?

In one round you saw the whole core loop: **straighten → build → fight → sneak → reset.** You spent Copper to arm a Hero and pressed a daytime attack that bled Rashida's Water; she answered with a midnight raid that stole yours right back. Neither of you lost a single card — but *life* sloshed back and forth across the table, and whoever runs the other dry first takes the game. That push-and-pull — Senpet's disciplined daylight assault against Moto's midnight banditry — is Legend of the Burning Sands in a nutshell.

## 12. Trait cheat-sheet

You'll see these bold keywords in card text. Quick translations:

| Trait | What it means |
|-------|---------------|
| **Sahir** | A spellcaster — bow one to cast a Spell. |
| **Jinn** | A summoned spirit-Hero ("smokeless fire"); 0 Influence, Nonhuman. |
| **Khadi** | A sorcerer-assassin of the Caliph; can claw back from the dead at Night for 3 Water. |
| **Duelist** | +1 to parry values in duels. |
| **Flying** | Unit can slip in during the Flying maneuvers step. |
| **Archery** | Aim engage damage at one specific enemy card. |
| **Berserk** | Opponent must absorb your damage with *everything*. |
| **Carry +X** | Steal X extra Water on a successful raid. |
| **Tactician** | Pump a Hero's Strength from your hand during a battle. |
| **Experienced** | A later version of a Hero you can upgrade into mid-game. |
| **Unique** | Only one copy in play at a time (you can still deck 3). |
| **Fortification** | A Holding that attaches to and protects a City Section. |
| **Advanced** | A City Section subtype some cards care about. |
| **Undead** | Nonhuman; won't raid or defend against raids. |

## 13. Your first game

Honestly? **Don't try to hold all of this in your head.** Pick a faction whose vibe you like, deal out your starter, put your Stronghold down, buy a couple of City Sections, and just start taking turns. Hire a Hero. Bow a Holding for Copper. Poke at an opponent's City Section and see what happens.

The rhythm — *straighten, build, fight, sneak, cleanup* — becomes second nature in a game or two, and then all these traits and tricks start to feel less like rules and more like options. That's when Legend of the Burning Sands really opens up: a game of patient scheming in a city counting down its final days.

Now go tell a story worth remembering.
