Deck Philosophy

The Silky Doll Gambit is built on a mechanical fact that most decks cannot replicate: every single starting Character already has the Seduction skill printed on their card. This matters more than it appears. In most Seduction-themed decks, Lingerie serves as the unlock — it grants Seduction to a character who does not have it. Here, all four characters already have Seduction natively, so Lingerie does not unlock the skill, it stacks on top of an existing skill instance, pushing the stat higher. Servant's number of uses equals the equipping character's Charm — and when that character has native Seduction, every Charm buff on the board feeds directly into how many times Servant fires. This is the engine: native skill ownership means every item, enhancement, and global effect compounds instead of merely enabling.

The structural foundation rests on two characters. Sylvie is the Charm anchor — CHR:6 is the highest confirmed Charm stat among the deck's characters, and she carries Pilot, Acrobatics, Computer, Driving, and Strength in addition to Seduction. Her immunity to the 33-S Sexaroid Disaster (which this deck runs in its own disaster suite) means she can safely engage that particular android threat without risk. Washu is the sustain engine — Energy:5 fuels expensive power effects, her passive grants +1 Health to every other character in the party, and Genius×2 plus Computer unlock dimensional and tech-gated locations. Together they form a core that is both hard to remove and continuously generating advantage.

Annapuna and Unipuma are the parallel combat axis. Neither one is particularly threatening alone at ATK:3/DEF:3, but the moment they occupy the same Location, Anna's DEF rises to 4 and Uni's ATK rises to 4. They become a DEF:4/ATK:4 pair who share identical skill profiles — Acrobatics, Streetwise, Seduction, Music — meaning they can access the same locations and scavenge the same Items. The Music skill opens concert and entertainment venues like Tokyo Mega Dome directly. Deploy them together, always, and the synergy activates immediately. This frees Sylvie to operate as a solo Charm engine while the pair handles physical combat on a second front.

Pillar 1 — Native Seduction StackAll four characters have Seduction built in. Lingerie stacks on existing skill instances rather than unlocking. Servant scales with Charm. Every buff compounds.
Pillar 2 — Sylvie Charm CeilingCHR:6 base. Stack Dollies, Jewels, Compact, Haven bonus to reach CHR:22. Enchanted View doubles it to 44. No opponent survives a resolved ×2 on a pre-stacked Charm total.
Pillar 3 — Disaster AsymmetryAngry Schoolgirls, Akane's Rage, and Miss Hinako all target males or specific characters. Your all-female team is entirely immune. Your own disasters roam freely on the opponent's side.

"Can not be attacked by the 33-S Disaster. An escaped 33-S Sexaroid Boomer."
Sylvie is the primary Charm combat engine. CHR:6 is the highest confirmed Charm stat among the four starting characters, and it is the number every item and enhancement in this deck is trying to multiply. Her physical stats are deliberately thin (ATK:2/DEF:2) — a signal that she was designed to win through Charm, and every card in this deck is built to let her do that. The immunity to the 33-S Sexaroid Disaster is a genuine tactical advantage: this deck runs the 33-S in its own disaster suite, so Sylvie can safely work around it while opposing physical characters get harassed by it. Her six-skill breadth — Pilot, Acrobatics, Computer, Driving, Strength, Seduction — opens more location access routes than any other character in the roster. Stack three Cute Little Dollies and three Wedding Jewels on her and she reaches CHR:18 before the Haven bonus or any combat card.

"All other characters in party are +1 Health."

Energy:5 is the highest Energy value in the deck, enabling expensive power card effects and Energy-cost items. Android-type character with Seduction built in means she never needs Lingerie to access Seduction-gated content. Unipuma is the offensive half of the pair. The identical skill profile to Annapuna (Acrobatics, Streetwise, Seduction, Music) means they can access the exact same locations, scavenge the same Items, and never need to split up to cover different territory. This is a feature, not a limitation — it means you always want them together, and since that is already the correct play for the synergy bonus, you never face a trade-off decision. Her Music skill alongside Anna's ensures both have native access to Tokyo Mega Dome without requiring any skill-granting equipment.

Haven — The Silky Doll
1 card
×1
The Silky Doll Set 0 Rare
Haven Bubblegum Crisis
"All Characters from this Haven may add +1 to Charm any given turn."
The Silky Doll is mechanically the only correct Haven for this deck. The +1 Charm bonus applies every turn to every Character — not once, not conditionally, but as a permanent structural uplift that compounds over the course of a game. Unlike Items (destroyable by Disasters) or Enhancements (occupy slots and can be targeted), the Haven's bonus is baseline. It cannot be stolen, discarded, or disrupted. Combined with Compact (+1 for females), Cute Little Dolly (+2 per copy), and Wedding Jewels (+2 per Enhancement), the Haven is the invisible foundation that makes the Charm mathematics possible. Over six turns across four characters, the Haven alone contributes 24 Charm to your board state that opponents never had to deal with. The thematic resonance with Sylvie — a Bubblegum Crisis android whose home bar this literally is — elevates the deck's coherence from mechanical to narrative.

The Seduction Package
11 items
×2
Lingerie Set 0 Common
Grants the Seduction skill. When a character already has Seduction, stacks the existing skill instance.
In most decks, Lingerie is the Seduction unlock. In this deck, all four characters already have native Seduction, so Lingerie does something more valuable: it stacks on top of the existing skill instance, increasing the Seduction stat rather than merely unlocking it. The mechanical distinction matters — stacking pushes the skill level higher, which affects how Seduction-scaled interactions resolve. Two copies means any two characters can carry a stacked Seduction at once, making the entire roster more dangerous in Charm-contested locations.
×3

+2 Charm.
The deck's primary Charm item. Three copies on Sylvie adds +6 Charm to her base CHR:6, bringing her to CHR:12 before any other enhancement, Haven bonus, or combat card. At Common rarity, three copies are easy to acquire. Always stack all three on Sylvie first — she is the Enchanted View delivery vehicle and needs the highest Charm ceiling possible before the finisher lands.
×1
Servant Set 0 Uncommon
"Number of uses is equal to Characters Charm."
Servant's power scales directly with the equipping character's Charm stat. With all four characters having native Seduction, any of them can equip Servant and immediately get uses equal to their current Charm total. On Sylvie at CHR:12+ mid-game, Servant becomes a massively efficient equipment fetcher — far more uses than any other character in the format could achieve. Equip it to whoever currently holds the highest Charm, and let it work. As the Charm stack builds through items and enhancements, Servant's use count climbs in lockstep.
×2

+1 Charm for female characters.
All four starting characters are female. Two Compacts means +2 Charm distributed across whoever needs it most. Low cost, reliable bonus, no conditional requirement beyond gender — which this deck satisfies entirely. Stack both on Sylvie for +2 more Charm toward the ceiling, or distribute one each to Washu and a Puma sister to make them more useful in Charm combat situations.

Every item, enhancement, and global effect in the deck feeds into a single Charm total on Sylvie. The math is not theoretical — every component listed below is a card actually in the deck, playable in a real game. The goal is to reach the Enchanted View trigger point, at which the doubler ends the combat outright.

Striptease Set 1 Uncommon
"Males are unable to Scavenge or help in Combat."
An all-female deck's secret weapon. Striptease locks every male character on the field out of scavenging and combat assistance for a full turn — and your team is entirely unaffected. Against male-heavy Physical decks this is a complete board stall: their attackers cannot engage in meaningful actions while your four female characters operate freely. Two copies means you can play this in consecutive turns against a deck that cannot recover. The card does not require a target — it is a field-wide effect that specifically exempts your roster.
×2
Bad Reputation Set 0 Rare
Reduce opponent's Charm by 2.
Charm debuff while you are actively Charm stacking. Two copies means -4 Charm to any opponent trying to fight you in the Charm dimension. Against rival Charm decks, Bad Reputation is the primary counter: they are building their total, you are building your total and simultaneously reducing theirs. The net swing of two copies is significant enough to flip matchups that would otherwise be close.
×1
Tender Moment Set 1 Rare
All Characters +1 Charm. Non-cumulative.
Field-wide +1 Charm. Non-cumulative means you only run one, which is all you need. Every character on your side of the board benefits — Washu, Annapuna, and Unipuma all get closer to meaningful Charm combat participation, while Sylvie's total climbs another point toward the Enchanted View threshold.
×1
Following Orders Set 1 Uncommon
"Reverse the result of 1 Charm combat."
Hold this as counterplay. If an opponent somehow out-charms you in a given round — through their own Enchanted View or a lucky combat card draw — Following Orders flips the result. It is a one-of because you should rarely need it, but when you do, it saves a character that would otherwise be eliminated. The mere knowledge that an opponent is running it changes how aggressively they commit to Charm combat finishers.

Angry Schoolgirls, Akane's Rage, and Miss Hinako all either target males specifically or have exploitable targeting — your team is never their victim.

Strategy Guide
Opening — Establish the Engine
Priority 1 — Silky Doll Haven Establish the Haven immediately. Every turn it is not in play is a missed +1 Charm per character. The Haven bonus is the invisible hand that makes all your math work — get it down before the first disaster hits.
Priority 2 — Stack Dollies on Sylvie All three Cute Little Dollies go on Sylvie first. CHR:6 + 6 = CHR:12 before any other enhancement fires. She is the Enchanted View delivery vehicle; everything else in the deck is building toward her pulling that trigger.
Priority 3 — Equip Servant Early Equip Servant to whoever currently has the highest Charm. Washu's +1 Health passive is already active the moment she is in the party — the three other characters benefit from turn one. Use Servant's uses to fish for Wedding Jewels and Combat Training.
Midgame — Deploy the Pair and Build the Stack
Anna + Uni — Always Together Deploy Annapuna and Unipuma to the same Location every time. Instant ATK:4/DEF:4 the moment they share a space. They handle physical combat on a second front while Sylvie builds Charm totals unopposed on the other side of the board.
Charm vs Physical Assignment Sylvie handles all Charm combat. Anna and Uni handle lower-threat physical targets at ATK:4. Washu stays close to the party for the +1 Health passive and takes any Medical Treatment usage. Never send Sylvie into physical combat if Charm combat is available.
Disaster Placement Place Angry Schoolgirls and Akane's Rage on opponent's side of the field near their male characters. They will roam toward male targets automatically — your team never gets chased. Place Akane's Cooking under a key location opponent needs.
Endgame — Execute the Charm Finisher
Sylvie at CHR:20+ — Game-Ending Window Once Sylvie reaches CHR:20 or higher, any Enchanted View play produces a CHR:40+ combat total. Use Briefing to sequence Enchanted View to the top of the draw pile. Play Handsome Lad first (+2/−1 swing), then Enchanted View. Combat ends.
Striptease + Disaster Lock In the same turn you plan to execute the Charm finisher, play Striptease. Male opponents cannot scavenge or assist in combat for that turn — they cannot counter your Charm sequence. Your female team is unaffected. This is the full board stall window.
Following Orders as Insurance Hold Following Orders until the Enchanted View turn. If the opponent somehow has an answer — their own Enchanted View, or a Panic that resets your Charm — Following Orders reverses the result after the fact. It is the insurance policy that makes aggressive commitment correct.
Matchup Tips
vs Physical Decks Striptease shuts them down if they run males. Gas Attack/Incomparable Beauty removes their physical defense entirely in any combat round. Combat Training on Sylvie brings her to ATK:5/DEF:5 if physical trades are unavoidable. Anna+Uni's ATK:4/DEF:4 pair handles cleanup.
vs Rival Charm Decks Bad Reputation ×2 debuffs them for -4 Charm. Following Orders reverses their best Charm swing. Your Charm ceiling (CHR:54 with Enchanted View) is structurally higher than any standard Charm build that lacks the all-female Seduction-native roster. Outlast and out-stack.
vs Disruption Decks Washu's +1 Health passive provides resilience before healing is needed. Medical Treatment ×2 and Refreshing Drink are the recovery tools. Angel of Mercy recovers lost key cards. Wrath of the Eye of God resets catastrophically large Disasters if the disruption deck snowballs a threat.
