Deck Philosophy

In Ani-Mayhem, the game is decided by one simple truth: whoever collects the most Items wins. Everything else — the combat, the Disasters, the power cards — is infrastructure serving that goal. The Armitage Protocol is built around three pillars:

01 — SpeedGet to Items before your opponents do. A-ko's MOV 4 sets the pace no other starting Character can match.
02 — Combat DominanceStack Combat Training to push every Character into Major-Disaster attack territory. Clear Disasters faster than anyone.
03 — ResilienceArmitage's Health 8 and revival clause mean your best attacker is never truly off the table.

A-ko Magami Set 1

A-ko is the fastest Character in the entire Set 0/1 cardpool. Movement 4 means she reaches Locations and Items a full turn ahead of most threats — she is your opener, racing toward Items while everyone else is still setting up. Her Strength ×3 is the real headline: a huge number of Locations across both sets require the Strength skill, and A-ko satisfies those requirements solo without needing teammates present. She doesn't just open doors — she kicks them in.

Naomi Armitage Set 1
ATK 4DEF 3MOV 3CHM 2 Health 8Revives Once PoliceInvestigationStrengthMarksman
Health 8. Can be brought back from Killed once per game.
Armitage is the toughest non-boss Character in either set. Health 8 means she absorbs dramatically more punishment than her DEF 3 alone implies — the difference between Bonked and Killed is the gap between 1 and negative base Health, and with 8 Health she rarely gets there. Her revival clause is a massive advantage: send Armitage at the hardest Disaster on the board. Even if she dies, she comes back. She is the deck's insurance policy and its most aggressive piece simultaneously.

Ryoko Set 0
ATK 4DEF 4MOV 3CHM 3NRG 4 PilotStreetwiseSeduction FlyingPhaseStrength
Will not attack Tenchi.
Ryoko is the most skill-diverse Character in Set 0. Six skills — including the rare Phase — make her exceptional. Phase lets her pass through Locations without stopping, a mobility trick no other standard Character replicates. Her NRG 4 powers every Energy-cost card in the deck. Most importantly: the Power Gem Item text specifically notes "Ryoko's gains +1 Attack" per gem — two Power Gems push her to ATK 6, then Tenchi the Master Key adds another +2 for ATK 8 base before any Enhancements fire.

ATK 5/2DEF 5/2MOV 3/2 TeacherClimbingSurvivalStrength ×2

Fujisawa is the highest base Attack of any normal Character in Set 0. ATK 5 when sober — which is 2 out of every 3 turns — is categorically above the curve. The drunk turn is predictable and manageable: don't put him in combat on turn three. His Strength ×2 stacks with A-ko's ×3 for multi-Strength Locations. Climbing and Survival open the El-Hazard mountain and desert Locations that other Characters can't reach.

Starting Haven
1 card
×1
Raven's Garage Set 0
Characters from this Haven do not lose Equipment when it is destroyed. Return cards to draw pile.
Haven choice is one of the most impactful deckbuilding decisions, and Raven's Garage earns its slot by solving the game's most frustrating problem: Equipment loss. When Characters are Bonked or Killed they drop their Equipment under the Location. Without Raven's Garage, that Equipment is either taken by opponents or buried under a Disaster. With it, your Leon's Boomer Rifles, K-12 Battlesuits, and Laser Pistols all cycle back into the Draw pile. Opponents cannot permanently strip your weapons — making targeting your Equipment a wasted action.

Items are the victory condition. Every one of these also provides meaningful in-game utility when carried — double-value picks that count toward the final tally and help your team win.

Power Gem Set 0
+1 to Energy stat. Limit 2 gems per Character. Ryoko's gains +1 Attack.
The most important Item in the deck. Each gem gives +1 Energy to whoever carries it. Ryoko specifically gains +1 Attack per gem — stack two gems on Ryoko for ATK 6 base, then add the Master Key for ATK 8. Three copies in the deck means at least one almost certainly surfaces on the Field during setup.
×1
Tenchi The Master Key Set 0
+2 Attack. Hold up to 3 Power Gems.
The centrepiece of the Ryoko combo. Master Key (+2 ATK) plus two Power Gems (+2 ATK on Ryoko) plus Combat Training ×3 (+3 ATK) equals Ryoko at ATK 11 base. Draw Smash from the combat pile and she deals 22 damage in a single round. Nothing in either set survives that.
×1
C-ko Kotobuki Set 1
While in possession minus 1 to all your Attacks. Counts as 2 Items for final Item tally.
The deck's most controversial inclusion. That -1 ATK penalty is real — never carry C-ko into combat. But she counts as 2 Items at game end. In a tight competitive game where 1 Item decides the winner, C-ko banking 2 points while sitting safely in your Haven is the margin of victory. Collect her last, hold her safe, win.

Power Cards

×3
Combat Training Set 0 Core Engine
All Characters gain +1 attack and +1 defense for each Combat Training in play up to 3.
This is the deck's engine. Not a single-Character buff — a global buff to every one of your Characters simultaneously. With three Combat Training cards in play, every Character gains +3 ATK and +3 DEF. A-ko jumps from ATK 4 to ATK 7. Fujisawa (sober) from ATK 5 to ATK 8. Getting all three Combat Training cards active is the deck's primary mid-game objective — everything else is secondary.
×3
Wrath of the Eye of God Set 0
Destroys any card in play except Havens.
The single most powerful Flash Effect in either set. Major Disaster about to detonate? Gone. Opponent's Combat Training stack? Gone. Three copies means you almost always have one in hand. Because Flash Effects resolve last-in-first-out, you can also counter an opponent's Wrath with your own Wrath before theirs resolves — removing it from the stack before it can destroy anything.

Strategy Guide

Opening Turns: Send A-ko immediately toward the nearest accessible Location — MOV 4 gets her there before anyone else. Ryoko handles Locations requiring Phase or Flying. Armitage and Fujisawa move together to handle Draw pile Disasters that emerge during Reload phases. During setup, place DDJ1 Battlemover and Dobermans over your highest-value Items (Power Gems, Master Key). Place Class C Boomers over Locations opponents might reach first.

Mid-Game Priority: Getting all three Combat Training cards into play is the deck's primary objective. Once the full stack is active, disperse Characters across separate Locations — every Character individually has the combat power to handle most Disasters solo. Use Briefing to sequence Combat Training to the top of your Draw pile. Use Dr. Stingray the moment it surfaces to nerf all Boomers on the board.

End Game: Track the Item count from mid-game onward. With 7 Locations, collecting 4 Items wins. C-ko Kotobuki's 2-for-1 value is critical in close games — she alone represents the margin between a tie and a win. Collect her last, after your combat-active Characters have cleared their Locations.

⚠️ Don't carry C-ko into combatHer -1 ATK penalty stacks badly with an already-buffed team. Let her sit, collect her last, bank 2 points.
🕐 Track Fujisawa's drunk cycleHe goes drunk every 3rd turn. Note when his last drunk turn was. Never commit him to combat on that turn.
⚡ Wrath counters WrathFlash Effects are last-in, first-out. Chain your Wrath immediately after an opponent plays theirs to destroy it before it resolves.
🔄 Never discard depleted RiflesRaven's Garage recycles destroyed Equipment. Reload the same Rifle across multiple Characters — it's functionally infinite.
🚫 Ryoko won't attack TenchiIf an opponent runs Tenchi Masaki, assign Armitage or A-ko to that fight. Don't assign Ryoko to a target she'll refuse to attack.
💉 Armitage can return 3 timesHer natural revival + Angel of Mercy + Elite Medical Staff = three returns from death in one game. She is effectively immortal.
